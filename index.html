FAQ

What is this for?

It makes "tap-to-reveal" images for X/Twitter — a picture that looks like one thing in the timeline, then shows the full image when you tap it. Built for that trend, but useful anywhere you want a hidden/revealed layer.

Why does it hide, then reveal?

X shows a shrunk-down preview in the feed and the full-resolution image only on tap. The "hidden" areas are stored as a sparse pattern of pixels with transparent gaps. Shrunk down, those pixels blend into the gaps and nearly disappear; at full size they show at full strength and the whole image appears.

Why PNG only — why not JPG?

The effect needs transparency and lossless pixels. PNG has both; JPG has no transparency and re-compresses the image, smearing the fine pattern. GIF and WebP get converted by X. An indexed PNG-8 is the one format that survives intact.

Why post only from a computer?

The mobile X apps re-encode uploads (often PNG→JPG), destroying the transparency. A desktop-browser upload on x.com keeps the PNG as-is.

Why the careful file-size limit?

X leaves a PNG untouched only if it's small enough; over the limit it re-compresses to JPG and breaks the effect. The export is auto-tuned to sit just under ~700 KB / 2500 px — small enough to stay PNG, big enough to stay sharp.
